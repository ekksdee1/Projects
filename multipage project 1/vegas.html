The Vegas Experience

Las Vegas is definitely not a city for kids. It's filled with tourists and (some) adult themes. But, it is also a very cool place to visit if you're of age (or mature enough). I mainly visited the Las Vegas strip, where all the 5-star hotels are at, as well as tourist attractions. It's also worth mentioning that the food there is to die for. From Gordon Ramsay owned restaurants to 5 star dining, it is definitely worth a trip if you can afford it.

Vegas turns into a completely different city at night. In the day, it's lively and an entertaining city, but at night it becomes this city with a huge party vibe. At night, all of the buildings and attractions are lit up, and the difference is just breathtakingâ€”especially if you have a good view from above.

The Las Vegas strip uses mainly taxis to transport everyone. As a result, the taxi buisness is thriving in that area, unlike how in our Toledo and Sylvania area, where Uber and whatnot has mostly replaced the need for taxis. This was very intriguing to me, as I had not seen an area before with a booming taxi buisness (besides airports).

I also visited the Hoover Dam, which is a little bit out of the way of Vegas, but it was very cool to see how the Dam worked, and it was also a very good picture.
