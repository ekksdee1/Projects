My Los Angeles Experience

LA is a very lively place, filled with tourists, locals, and all sorts of people! The place I visited specifically was Santa Monica. It had many nice neighborhoods, but the houses were very small. That's one thing I noticed about the big cities in California, unless you're very rich, you don't get a big house with a big yard unlike what we have in Sylvania or even Toledo.

Some of the attractions that I visited were Universal Studios and the Santa Monica Pier. Universal Studios is a theme park/amusement park that doesn't really have any roller coasters; it's more about illusions that make you think you're moving really fast, but the actual ride isn't. I will admit: the rides felt very real, even if I knew we weren't actually moving. The Santa Monica Pier is kind of like a fair with many restaurants, amusement rides, a ferris wheel, et cetera.

I also visited The Aquarium of the Pacific, as well as the Hollywood Sign. The Aquarium of the Pacific is a very famous aquarium in LA, I saw many aquatic animals, such as penguins, stingrays, sea lions, seals, and even small sharks! The Hollywood sign was a cool place to take a picture, but there wasn't much actually at the Hollywood sign. Actual Hollywood was pretty cool, and you could definitely tell the houses were meant for rich people.
